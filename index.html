After the Agricultural Revolution, more food was available which increased the population (more food meant lower price of goods which meant more babies could survive without the fear of dying of hunger). Moreover, the risk of famine decreased as newer farming methods that were made ensured a more stable yield every year, which also contributed to a decrease in deaths every year due to hunger.

Soon after the Agricultural Revolution, the Energy Revolution started. Instead of Human and Animal power, people ventured out for better and more efficient methods of getting energy such as Water & Wind Mills, Giant Water Wheels, Coal, and eventually people invented the Steam Engine. People used these advancements for creating the textile industry and revolutionizing the transportation industry forever.

Britain led the Industrial Revolution because their climate was good, they had natural resources, encouraged internal trade, and because of their divisions of regions into colonies each colony supplied raw materials found specifically in their region. Moreover, Britain had a monopoly on their technology since they didn't allow engineers that helped with inventing new ideas to further industrialization to leave the country. With this and a capitalistic economy, Britain created the Textile Industry which pressured the invention of the Spinning Jenny that spun many threads at once and many other inventions.

Effects of the Creation of the Textile Industry

  • Mass production of textiles dropped prices significantly.

  • Due to the substantial influx of workers moving near rivers in a small amount of time, people who lived near rivers (where textile factories run on water power stayed) had to move because of the pollution.

Soon after the Spinning Jenny was invented, the steam engine was invented which had a lasting impact on the industrial revoltion. With the steam engine, there was a demand for metals and coal.

Effects of the Steam Engine

  • Revolutionized Transportation Industry

  • Increased demand for coal and metals which Britain had a lot of in the colonies.

Revolution in Transportation during the Industrial Revoltuion

  • Turnpikes, Canals, and Stronger bridges were made to make transportation via steamboat and trains easier.

  • The invention of the steam locomotive grew the number of railroads substantially in a small period of time. In a decade most of England was accessible via locomotion.

Due to these reasons:

Effects of Transportation

  • Goods were cheaper since they were easier to transport
  • People could travel further to meet one another easier and faster
  • Increased trading which increased the economy

Downside of the Revolution

Although Britain's economy increased, communication increased among people, more people had jobs, and people could afford more, there were many downsides to the revolution.

  • Since factories that were powered by water were established near rivers, people started moving into densly populated areas were work was available (like the factories). This caused noise, air, and river pollution and a much greater gap between the rich and poor.

  • Neighborhoods where the working and lower class lived was terrible. Generally there wasn't any light or running water meaning that there was no sewage which threatened the sanitation of people greatly. This meant that disease could spread quicker among the densely populated population.

      • Factories themselves had problems as well.

    • Working conditions of the working class were horrible with 12-16 working hours and no lung protection from harmfull gasses which could cause lung cancer and other lung related problems.

    • Women workers were paid less than men and when they were working they generally spent long times away from their families.

    • Due to protests and other factors, factory owners labored children since they worked quickly and were managable.

    • The children had to work 12-16 gruelsome working hours like adults and were paid less. Children were also abused more often since they weren't strong enough to fight back. Children were nearly treated like slaves by being punished not to show up for work.
